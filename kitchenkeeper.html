Kitchen Keeper

Food's better fresh

Overview

Problem

While consumers are aware of food expiration, working professionals feel as if they are too busy to consistently track their food in their kitchen. Sell by dates and expirations are not considered relevant due to their prior experience of eating foods past their best by dates and not feeling sick.

We want to build an app that helps the user keep track expiration dates for the food they purchase and bring into their home.

How might we build an easy and convenient way to be informed about the foods that you buy and notify you when the foods in your kitchen are a few days frome expiration?

Solution

Kitchen Keeper is a convenient tool that tracks and informs kitchen food’s lifespan for busy working professionals who need help keeping track of the shelf life of the food they purchase will achieve less food waste within their kitchens and increase knowledge of food safety with the Kitchen Keeper app.

Interviews & Affinity Mapping

During the research phase, we sought to understand how working professionals cook, track, and prioritize their food. We also needed to uncover the needs and frustrations of our target user when they track their food to better understand how to build a solution.



Research Goals

  • Understand to what extent working professionals, who cook, make purchasing decisions regarding the lifespan of the food they buy.

  • Understand how working professionals decide which foods to cook during the week

  • Understand to what extent do working professionals track the expiration dates on the food in their kitchens.


Primary Research - Interviews


To better understand the perspective of our target user, we interviewed 9 active people who regularly shop at grocery stores.

Research Assumptions

  • Tracking food is used by your memory and people lack tech resources to track their foods.

  • Users are frustrated when they throw away spoiled food.

  • People would prefer to eat all their purchases instead of throwing it out.


After completion of the interviews, we put the responses on sticky notes and clustered them into an Affinity Map. This process helped us organize and synthesize our interviewees responses and user themes we found.


Key Findings

  • Users use their senses of touch and smell to determine if their food is still good.

  • Users do not consider sell-by dates relevant and they do not track expiration dates.

  • Users track their food based on their memory.

Competitive Analysis

We performed a competitive analysis to understand the different competitors in our space. We wanted to understand their strengths, weaknesses, similarities, and differences and how the users reacted to their application.






Our competitive analysis produced the following insights:

  • There are not that many competitors in the space that track food on a mobile app. This showed us an opportunity to create a mobile app to improve the efficiency of tracking food in the kitchen.
  • Build an easier flow for beneficiaries to request a foodbox or recieve
  • Connecting to a grocery store API will save time when uploading or saving food in your profile. Additionally, scanning the barcode of the food seemed to be a user favorite feature.
  • There are food management apps, and weight loss apps, but no apps that track how long food has been in your kitchen.

User Flow





When we ideated our user flow, we considered how our users may utilize our app we thought of three main information points.




  • Onboarding, need to be intuitive and informational to understand the unique nature of our app
  • The adding and removing of products in an efficient manner so users will not feel burdened.
  • Notifications that are timely and relevant to the users needs as well as an option to share information with others in the household.

Usability Feedback & Revised Designs

We conducted 9 user testsThe participants were given 4 scenarios each with a task to complete. Our Usability testing focused on three objectives:

  • Onboard
  • Add an Item
  • Remove an Item
  • Find your grocery list



From our testing we concluded that our onboarding needed more clarification. There was concern about manual input of information when adding an Item and we found an easier way to remove products from the application.

Mid-Prototype



Among all visual and experiential changes we have made in iterating on our original wireframes, we as a group had focused our efforts on changes regarding:

  • Onboarding the user
  • Adding / Removing food item
  • Setting notifications


Future Steps

If our team had more time, we would work on detailing the notifications, this would allow our users to customize the notifications for their needs. We have also been exploring the idea of fully rolling out the “scan the barcode” of the food item feature to streamline the upload of the app.
