Growhaus

Food Equality . NonProfit. Sustainable.

Overview

Problem

While the residents of Denver are primarily aware of healthy foods, there are still counties of Denver that experience food insecurity, which is the state of being without reliable access to a sufficient quantity of affordable, nutritious food. This specific area in Denver (80216) is severely underserved by grocers and produce retailers that provide local, sustainably-sourced produce at an affordable cost.

GrowHaus Denver was designed to achieve affordable, sustainable, and fresh groceries to its Denver community. We have observed that our client’s current webpage isn’t converting visitors to donars and it is a complicated site to navigate to if you are a recipient of a foodbox. This resulted to a lack of funds and confused beneficiaries who are left to call the team to order their foodbox instead of going through the online process, creating long waits on the telephone lines.



How might we improve the user experience by redesigning the website’s user interface in effort to (1) streamline CTA to purchase or donate (2) improve the marketing reach of the organization and brand awareness in the Denver-area so that donations reach families and families reach food boxes.

Solution

We redesigned three different CTA flows on the Growhaus website: the donation path, requesting foodboxes path, and customizing food boxes. It also enables users to switch between an EN and ES website and also contact customer service through email and phone.

Interviews & Affinity Mapping

To better understand the technology issues that Growhaus faces, I reached out to Growhaus' Director of Business, Nathan, to help us understand their pain points, what is going well, and what they hope to achieve.



  • Improve their communication regarding their stance with COVID and BLM

  • Get more people to donate

  • Get more foodbox orders



Now, we had to discover and understand how a nonprofit website communicates its mission and services as well as tactics to encourage website visitors to take action by donating or volunteering.


We interviewed around 10 peole who have engaged, donated, or recieved support from one or more Nonprofits in the last 3 years. We found:

br/>
  • Users need an easy call to action to feel engaged with a non-profit

  • Users want to see the mission statement and who to contact on the homepage

  • Users want to see where donations are going with real anecdotes and testimonials


We administered 8 usability tests on the Growhaus Website on three platforms - mobile, web, and iPad.




Because of Growhaus' hard to find Call To Actions, hidden mission statement, and lack of easy to find resources to deem their credibility, visitors do not stay long on the Growhaus website, resulting in a loss opportunity to gain more donations for foodboxes for residents of 80212.



All this information helped us shape our problem statement.

Usability Feedback & Revised Designs

Our Usability testing focused on three objectives:

  • User's ability to order a foodbox
  • User's understanding of the purpose of the GrowHaus
  • User's ability to donate a foodbox





We found we still had a lot of work to do on the user flow for donating and ordering a foodbox. People were still a little lost on where they were on the screens and did not feel the brand was consistent on each page.

Since the goal of our redesign is to help visitors of the site have an easier time donating or ordering their foodbox, we decided to completely redo our designs and start from scratch provided the feedback. We focused our attention and scaled back on what the process would look like when ordering a food from each intentional interaction. What would the users be thinking? What types of small details will help them feel like they are going in the right direction?
